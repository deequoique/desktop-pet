# Screen Dog Look Mechanics

- Preserve the square monitor head, two floppy ears, single central pedestal, one wide base, and connected mouse-tail in every direction.
- Direction is led by pupils and soft eyebrows inside the screen. The nose and mouth may shift slightly in the same direction without changing the cute identity.
- Keep the monitor face broadly frontal. Use only a restrained screen-plane turn for left and right; never rotate or skew the whole sprite.
- For up and down, move the eyes clearly toward the top or bottom edge and use a small matching ear response.
- The mouse is the physical tip of the flexible tail. The complete cord and mouse may counterbalance the gaze, but must remain connected.
- Keep the single pedestal and base registered at a stable scale and baseline across all 16 directions.
- No legs, extra feet, text, arrows, degree labels, motion marks, shadows, or detached effects.
